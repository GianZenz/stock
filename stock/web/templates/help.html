Strategy Guide

Language: EN IT

What the parameters mean

  • Fast SMA: A short moving average (e.g., 20 or 50) tracking recent price. Smaller = more sensitive.
  • Slow SMA: A long moving average (e.g., 50 or 200) tracking the long-term trend. Larger = smoother.
  • Top N: How many highest-scoring symbols to show after ranking.
  • Cache TTL: How long to reuse downloaded data before fetching again.
  • Throttle: Delay between requests to avoid rate limits.

How “BUY” is decided

The app checks three trend conditions:

  • Latest bullish crossover: Fast SMA crosses above Slow SMA.
  • Price is above Slow SMA (e.g., 200-day) — trend confirmation.
  • Fast SMA slope is positive — short-term uptrend.

BUY if at least 2 out of 3 pass; otherwise DON'T BUY.

Suggested starting points

  • Conservative trend: Fast=50, Slow=200. Fewer signals, more stable.
  • Responsive trend: Fast=20, Slow=50. More signals, more noise.
  • Daily data: Cache TTL=24h, Throttle≈400ms; increase if you hit rate limits.

Tips

  • Use Yahoo source for quick exploration (no API key).
  • Increase Top N once performance feels good; the cache speeds up repeats.
  • Combine the BUY decision with your own risk rules (position sizing, stops).
